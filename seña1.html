What does "I Love You" mean in Sign Language?

The sign "I love you" in sign language is a combination of three letters: "I" (for I), "L" (for love), and "Y" (for you). This sign is used to express love and affection towards another person.

How is the sign made?

To perform the sign "I love you" in American Sign Language (ASL):

  1. Raise your dominant hand.
  2. Extend your thumb and pinky while keeping your other fingers (index and middle) bent towards your palm.
  3. Your palm should be facing the person you are signing to.

Importance of the sign

The sign "I love you" is a beautiful form of communication that can be used not only by deaf individuals but also by anyone who wishes to express their feelings in a visual and accessible way.
